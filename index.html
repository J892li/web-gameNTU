Interactive Game Deisgn 

How do people portrait themselves in a modern society?

Our game, The Falling Angel, is a first person dialogue game to tell users the hidden story behind a serious social issue：Campus crime.

Our main character is a part-time Youtber.  Her death is closely related to the nature of her job, which is an internet celebrity who needs to take  selfies every day. How can a simple selfie killed her in the end?

Perhaps the reason we feel the need to take another, and yet another selfie, is in part that we as the surrealists wrote in 1928 never seem able to create a photo that will ‘fully correspond to what you want to see in yourself.’ (Rettberg, 2018) ​​​

My roles &  jobs：

1. Create a team charter

2. Assign roles to group members

3. Particpate in cutscenes shotting procedure

4. Code the game

5. User testing & UI optimization


Results：We coded 18 scenes in the end,  including 5  draft design, 2 times of user testing and finally over 85% of user satisfaction.

Challenges & Solutions

  1. How to create a sympathetic story line  -  Researched & ideate based on current game/ literature/ script, then curate the content as our own.
  2. No time for CG — video shooting -  Shoot videos and invite our group members to play the roles instead of doing game animation to save time and costs.
  3. Never used game engine before - Code the game - Researched and learned from scratch, identify similar features from other games then recreate our codes.
